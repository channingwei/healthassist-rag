COMMON SYMPTOMS AND WHEN TO SEEK MEDICAL ATTENTION

FEVER
- Normal body temperature: 97-99°F (36-37°C)
- Low-grade fever: 100-101°F (37.8-38.3°C)
- High fever: 103°F (39.4°C) or higher
- Seek immediate care if: fever above 104°F (40°C), fever lasting more than 3 days, or fever with severe symptoms

HEADACHE
- Common causes: stress, dehydration, lack of sleep, eye strain
- Seek medical attention if: sudden severe headache, headache with fever and stiff neck, headache after head injury, or persistent headaches

COUGH
- Acute cough: usually resolves within 3 weeks
- Chronic cough: lasts more than 8 weeks
- Seek medical attention if: cough with blood, cough lasting more than 3 weeks, or cough with difficulty breathing

FATIGUE
- Common causes: lack of sleep, stress, poor nutrition, sedentary lifestyle
- Seek medical attention if: persistent fatigue affecting daily life, fatigue with other concerning symptoms

NAUSEA AND VOMITING
- Common causes: food poisoning, viral infections, motion sickness
- Seek medical attention if: severe or persistent vomiting, vomiting with severe abdominal pain, or signs of dehydration

DIARRHEA
- Acute diarrhea: usually resolves within 1-2 days
- Seek medical attention if: diarrhea lasting more than 2 days, severe abdominal pain, blood in stool, or signs of dehydration

CHEST PAIN
- Seek immediate medical attention for any chest pain
- Could indicate: heart attack, angina, pulmonary embolism, or other serious conditions

SHORTNESS OF BREATH
- Seek immediate medical attention if: sudden onset, severe, or accompanied by chest pain
- Common causes: anxiety, asthma, respiratory infections

DIZZINESS
- Common causes: dehydration, low blood pressure, inner ear problems
- Seek medical attention if: severe, persistent, or accompanied by other neurological symptoms

JOINT PAIN
- Common causes: overuse, arthritis, injury
- Seek medical attention if: severe pain, swelling, redness, or limited range of motion

SKIN RASHES
- Common causes: allergies, infections, contact dermatitis
- Seek medical attention if: widespread rash, rash with fever, or rash that doesn't improve

EMERGENCY SYMPTOMS - SEEK IMMEDIATE CARE:
- Chest pain or pressure
- Difficulty breathing
- Severe bleeding
- Loss of consciousness
- Severe head injury
- Sudden severe headache
- Paralysis or weakness on one side
- Seizures
- Severe allergic reactions

Remember: This information is for general guidance only. Always consult healthcare professionals for proper diagnosis and treatment.
